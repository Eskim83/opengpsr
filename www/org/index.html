1. What is OpenGPSR?

OpenGPSR is a community-driven database aggregating information about economic operators and public contact points in the context of Regulation (EU) 2023/988 (GPSR – General Product Safety Regulation).

The project serves as a reference point for sellers, distributors, and e-commerce system integrators who need manufacturer identification and contact data, as well as the Responsible Person in the EU (if applicable). Each record is versioned, linked to a source, and maintains an informational verification status.

Note: OpenGPSR does not interpret regulations and does not ensure legal compliance — it provides standardized technical data for integration.

2. What OpenGPSR is NOT

To avoid operational and legal misconceptions, please assume that OpenGPSR:

  • Is NOT an official EU or national registry – it has no official status or authority authorization.
  • Does NOT guarantee completeness or accuracy – data may be incomplete or outdated.
  • Does NOT ensure legal compliance – using this data does not exempt you from verification.
  • Is NOT legal advice – it is a technical tool, not a legal interpretation.

4. FAQ

Is OpenGPSR an official GPSR registry?
No. OpenGPSR is a private open-source initiative. It is not an official government registry.
Is GPSR a company registry?
No. GPSR is a regulation. OpenGPSR is a contact database to help automate processes.
Where does the data come from?
From public sources (entity websites, documents, registries) and community submissions, which must have a verifiable source.
Is the data always up to date?
There is no such guarantee. Check the verification status and date. Data may be marked as OUTDATED / HISTORICAL / DISPUTED.
Who is responsible for data usage?
The end user. OpenGPSR is an informational tool, without warranty.
Why isn't all data public?
Some data may be insufficiently verified or restricted for safety/privacy reasons. Verification statuses are informational metadata.

5. Project Manifesto

  • Data as a common good: Safety information without paywalls.
  • Technology Neutrality: Open formats (JSON, CSV).
  • Source Transparency: Every record points to its source and history.
  • Distributed Responsibility: We encourage corrections based on evidence.
  • Context Primacy: Roles are contextual (Manufacturer / Importer / Responsible Person etc.).
